Jan 12th - Apr 9th, 2015


During these three months I spent between 60 and 80 hours per week in intensive instruction and independant work.


This was a fullstack course which covered HTML and CSS, Bootstrap and Handlebars, JavaScript and Ruby, but above everything else, it was an introduction into how to work intensly, learn and teach myself.


Montserrat College of Art

2005 - 2007


Immediately after highschool I attended a diminuitively small arts conservatory thirty minutes north of Boston.


It was a spectacular experience through which I learned and grew as an artist and as an individual. Being no stranger to intense schedules and rigirous work ethic, and having pleanty of credits to transfer in, I graduated in just two and a half years. During the bulk of my time there I was planning on persuing a double major in Painting and Printmaking, but in the end realized that I could shave a full year off by selecting just one. I graduated in two and a half years having completed all the requirements for both majors save repeating the senior seminar (which is primarily studio time).


I graduated with the Dean's Highest Honors.


North Carolina School of the Arts

2003 - 2007


NCSA is a residential highschool with an intense art focus.


At the age of 17 I left home and enrolled. It was one of the most intense educational experiences of my life, and prepared me to love a life of educational persuit.

Interests

I have lots of interests.


I dig on social justice, combating economic inequality and figuring out how to extend access to resources and opportunity to those who may not have easy access otherwise. Technology seems like a perfect extension of this. Politics are atrophying and the power of tech to affect the world is growing exponentially. So lets do this thing.


I love hiking and physical activity. I hiked the Appalachian Trail in 2010. South-bound. For 2,189 miles. On foot.


Gardening and growing food. I spent lots of tyme in my garden in North Carolina and feel kind of chagrined about how little access to garden space I've had here in California, where the growing season never ends.


There have been large periods of my life where I contra-danced like an addict. I love blues dancing and waltz. Would love to find those communities to plug into here, but have just maintained a level of busy/distracted since I've been here.

Art

I have had a life long love of creating, emoting, beauty and elegance. In addition to using dance as a primary outlet, I also studied painting and printmaking at an arts conservatory just to the north of Boston. Upon graduation, and entering the grind of regular life, my art production has slowed down significantly, but here is some of my work.


I constructed a printing press using a car jack, a platen and some angle-iron which allowed me to continue producing relief prints (wood cuts), and that became my primary love for a few years. Now I have made a commitment to acknowledging beauty and capturing it by way of a black and white photo every single day.
